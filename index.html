Melbourne Airbnb Market

Airbnb, short for "Air Bed and Breakfast", is a hospitality company that operates an online platform that allows users to rent out short-term stays and experiences. Since its beginning in 2008, Airbnb has become a powerhouse in the hospitality industry. It has given users cheaper alternatives property as well provide luxury product offerings. Using Airbnb Open Data compiled on 07 Dec 2018, we will further explore and discover what Airbnb has to offer in Melbourne.

Where To Find Airbnb in Melbourne?

As expected for a large city such as Melbourne, most Airbnb listings are centered around the CBD. The density of listings decreases as the distance away from the CBD increases.

However, this does not apply to pricing of listings. Prices varies from neighbourhood to neighbourhood with no apparent pattern. This could be attributed to the fact that many of Melbourne's most popular tourist destination are far from the CBD and is spread around all of Melbourne.

Further Breakdown of Property Type

When closely examining different neighbourhoods, Melbourne, Port Phillip and Yarra has the most Airbnb listings.

For overall offerings, the most popular one is entire home or apartment followed by private room and then shared room. Shared room is significantly less popular than the other two offerings. Despite this, some neighbourhood's room type offering does not follow this overall trend. For neighbourhoods such as Monash, Wyndham and Manningham, private room offerings outweights entire room or apartment offerings.

How Much Should You Pay?

For many travellers, one of the most important factor in determining where to visit is price for accommodation. When examing prices closer by neighbourhoods, it is clear that Yarra Ranges, Casey and Bayside are the most expensive. This could be due to a few factors:

  • It could be a tourism hotspot
  • Property prices for the area is high
  • Close proximity to Melbourne CBD
  • Close proximity to coastal areas and beaches

Do You Get What You Pay For?

With so many options available, a lot of travellers would look at reviews to determine if a place is worth it. Not only that, some travellers would also be more willing to pay more for a place that has better reviews.

The heatmap (left) highlights that for Melbourne a lot of listings are around $100 per night but is still score very high for their review. However, it is important to note that in the lower price bracket, review scores varies all the way from 20-25 to 95-100 range. Generally, higher priced listings does have better reviews.
